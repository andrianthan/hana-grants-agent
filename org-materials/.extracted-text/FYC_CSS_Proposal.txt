Hanna Center – Parent Education - Coursework
C. Proposal Narrative
1. Program Design
a. Executive Overview
Hanna Center proposes to deliver high-quality Parent Education – Coursework (Parent Ed)
services focused on a distinct and underserved population: caregivers of adolescents and
transitional-age youth (ages 12+), including teen parents. Drawing on more than 75 years
of experience working with system-involved youth and their families, Hanna brings deep
expertise in adolescent development, trauma-informed care, and family systems, making it
uniquely suited to deliver developmentally appropriate parenting education that supports
both immediate case plan goals and long-term family outcomes.
This program directly supports Sonoma County’s goals of preventing future maltreatment
and supporting reunification by strengthening the protective capacity of caregivers.
Through evidence-based instruction and individualized planning, Hanna equips parents
with practical tools to reduce conflict, improve communication, and foster stable,
supportive home environments that promote safety and healing.
The program is structured around the Triple P – Positive Parenting Program. Hanna’s
clinical and educational teams will deliver an evidence-based, trauma-informed curriculum
through flexible formats: group, individual, and virtual sessions. The model emphasizes
fidelity, participant engagement, and individualized goal-setting aligned with FYC case plan
requirements and the Strengthening Families Protective Factors framework, and is
implemented with fidelity to Triple P in alignment with FFPSA evidence-based
expectations.
Hanna’s program is built for access and responsiveness. Courses are offered weekdays
during regular business hours and weekday evenings and facilitated by bilingual, bicultural
staff. Services are offered on-site at Hanna’s Sonoma Valley campus, virtually, and at
community partner locations. A dual-facilitator model ensures each cohort receives clinical
depth and instructional consistency.
While Hanna is requesting a portion of available funding, its focused approach will
maximize value by meeting a critical need in the Parenting Education Network. This
specialized offering complements the work of existing providers serving families with
young children and expands the County’s capacity to support families navigating the
challenges of parenting adolescents. With capacity to serve up to 60-80 caregivers annually,
Hanna offers Sonoma County a trusted, experienced partner for delivering flexible,
evidence-based, and developmentally tailored parenting education services. Hanna will
serve as a highly responsive partner to FYC, with rapid intake, reliable documentation in
Apricot, and clear progress summaries that support timely court and case planning needs.

1

b. Process Upon Receiving Referrals
Referrals from Family, Youth & Children’s (FYC) Services are reviewed within two business
days by an intake coordinator. Staff confirm referral details and contact the referring social
worker if additional information is needed. A Spanish bilingual Community Health Worker
(CHW) reaches out by phone, text, or email, based on the parent’s preferences. The CHW
explains the course, addresses questions, and schedules intake or orientation to occur
within 7 calendar days of successful contact whenever possible, with expedited scheduling
available for court-ordered or time-sensitive cases. At least three contact attempts are
made within a two-week period, with all outreach tracked in the County’s designated data
system (currently Apricot), or any future CDSS-required system. If a referral is not
appropriate or if the family cannot participate, Hanna communicates with the County to
coordinate redirection.
c. Client Engagement
Hanna Center’s approach to client engagement is rooted in trauma-informed, culturally
responsive, and relationship-based practices. When a parent is referred to the Parent Ed
program, they are welcomed by a CHW who is bilingual, bicultural, and trained in
engagement strategies specific to families who are system-involved.
Intake is offered in person, by phone, or virtually, and CHWs use strategies rooted in
Motivational Interviewing (MI) to increase caregiver confidence and autonomy—especially
for those hesitant to participate. Hanna also works with FYC and other partners to help
address barriers like transportation, scheduling conflicts, or competing obligations.
CHWs offer session reminders, check-ins, and make-up options for missed classes. If
engagement barriers persist, the CHW initiates an escalation pathway that may include
case coordination with the referring social worker, flexible scheduling options, and
individualized support to prevent dropout while maintaining caregiver choice and dignity.
Re-engagement for caregivers who miss a class provides flexibility and is responsive to each
caregiver’s situation.
d. Intake and Assessment Process
Intake is conducted in the caregiver’s preferred language (Spanish or English), in person, by
phone, or virtually, and is led by a trained CHW. The process builds trust, clarifies goals, and
ensures alignment with the family’s child welfare case plan. Clinicians support as needed.
Staff confirm referral details, assess parenting context (e.g., custody, co-parenting,
reunification), and explore caregiver goals. Using MI, staff help caregivers identify their
priorities and potential barriers such as transportation or scheduling. A brief
screening—typically the Parenting Stress Index or Protective Factors Survey—assesses
parenting stress, communication, and confidence. The Protective Factors Survey also
informs goal setting and is re-administered at program completion to evaluate progress.
CHWs or clinicians integrate insights from FYC to develop a personalized participation plan
reflecting both caregiver voice and case plan objectives. Documentation is entered into the

2

County or CDSS-required system. Throughout intake and enrollment, Hanna maintains
communication with FYC, sharing updates on attendance, participation, goal progress, and
safety concerns in accordance with County confidentiality standards.
e. Goal Establishment
Each caregiver’s individualized participation plan is developed at intake and aligns with
their goals, the referring social worker’s case plan, and the Strengthening Families
Protective Factors framework. Priority areas—such as communication, co-parenting, youth
mental health, and reunification—are tied to Protective Factors like resilience, social
support, and parenting knowledge.
These goals are documented in the County system and revisited throughout the program to
guide facilitation. In group settings, they help tailor examples and discussions to lived
caregiver experiences.
At course completion, staff review progress, administer the Protective Factors
Retrospective Survey, and share updates with FYC on attendance, participation, and goal
progress. Outcomes will be reported using a Results-Based Accountability (RBA) lens: “How
much did we do? How well did we do it? Is anyone better off?” Metrics include attendance,
satisfaction, and growth on pre/post measures.
f. Duration of Services
Each cycle of Hanna Center’s Parent Ed program includes 8-10 weekly sessions, typically 90
minutes in length, delivered in group or individualized formats based on caregiver needs
and availability. Hanna will work with FYC’s team to determine if additional support
sessions are needed to meet caregiver goals or case plan timelines, while maintaining
completion of all required curriculum modules. Regardless of pacing or format, all
participants complete the same required curriculum modules and learning objectives, with
fidelity maintained through standardized lesson plans and facilitator checklists.
Make-up sessions and supplemental materials are available for missed classes, and rolling
enrollment or condensed 1:1 sessions may be offered for families with time-sensitive case
requirements (e.g., court orders, trial home visits).
g. Staffing
The Parent Ed program will be delivered by a cross-functional team from Hanna Center’s
Mental Health Hub (MHH) and Research and Training (R&T) departments. In person and
virtual courses are co-facilitated by a trainer and a CHW or clinician based on the goals and
needs of the participants.
● Clinician (0.1 FTE): A licensed or pre-licensed mental health professional supports
intake, goal-setting, and curriculum delivery with a focus on relational and
developmental needs.
● Trainer (0.1 FTE): A staff member from R&T or a similarly qualified educator leads
curriculum facilitation, ensuring content is accessible and engaging.

3

● CHW (0.2 FTE): A bilingual, bicultural CHW leads outreach, attendance tracking,
and participant support throughout the course.
● Intake Coordinator (.1 FTE): Staff member from MHH who makes initial contact
with all referred clients connecting them to program staff.
● Program Manager (0.1 FTE): Consultation and supervision of clinical services to
ensure fidelity administratively and in direct service care.
All referred clients will be directly served by the Intake Coordinator and CHW. Caregivers
who engage in the program will be directly served by the Trainer and Clinician. The
Program Manager will serve all caregivers indirectly and may provide direct services to
some. To ensure continuity and scheduling flexibility, Hanna will maintain cross-coverage
by training multiple staff in Triple P delivery and documentation procedures, allowing
rapid substitution if staffing or participant scheduling needs change.
h. Service Offering Location(s)
Hanna Center will deliver Parent Ed through a flexible, multi-site model with a focus on
accessibility:
● On-site at Hanna’s Sonoma Valley campus, accessible by public transit and with
trauma-informed, ADA-compliant meeting rooms, bilingual staff, and ample parking
● Off-site at trusted community partner locations (e.g., local schools, clinics, or
community centers)
● Virtual via HIPAA-compliant platforms (e.g., Zoom), with bilingual facilitation,
printed/emailed materials, and basic tech support; when appropriate, services may
also be delivered in-home or at another mutually agreed accessible location.
i. Evidence-Based Practices
Hanna Center will fully integrate the Triple P – Positive Parenting Program to enhance its
trauma-informed foundation with a structured, evidence-based model tailored to
caregivers of adolescents and transitional-age youth. Triple P complements—not
replaces—Hanna’s culturally responsive, family-centered approach, offering consistent,
measurable outcomes while maintaining flexibility and relational depth.
Triple P improves program consistency, aligns with FYC case plan requirements, supports
caregiver behavior change, and is scalable as multiple staff receive certification. Courses
will be delivered across group, individual, and virtual formats by certified clinician–trainer
teams using standardized materials.

2. Quality of Services
a. Unique Features of Proposal
Hanna Center’s Parent Ed program focuses on caregivers of adolescents and
transitional-age youth (12+), including teen parents. Core program strengths include:
1. Adolescent-Centered Focus
Hanna brings decades of experience supporting older youth and their families, offering
developmentally specific, trauma-informed parenting education tailored to the challenges
of parenting teens.
2. Dual-Facilitator Model
Each course is co-facilitated by a trainer and clinician or CHW, blending structured
instruction with therapeutic insight. This model allows for flexibility, individualized
support, and delivery across group, 1:1, and virtual formats.
3. System Integration
Hanna’s strong ties with schools, health centers, and County systems (e.g., Probation,
Health Services) ensure seamless referrals, warm handoffs, and coordinated reporting. Staff
are trained in Apricot and FYC protocols.
4. Built-In Fidelity Infrastructure
The R&T team oversees curriculum design and fidelity monitoring. Courses follow a
consistent structure with clear objectives, required modules, and adaptation points,
ensuring high-quality, consistent delivery across all formats.
5. Accountability and Continuity
Caregiver progress is tracked through pre/post measures and shared with FYC staff. Hanna
remains engaged post-course to support referrals and next steps, reinforcing continuity of
care.
b. Coordination and Communication
Hanna Center prioritizes close coordination with FYC Services to align parenting education
with case goals and system outcomes. Drawing on experience collaborating with County
agencies—including Probation, Health Services, and school districts—Hanna ensures
effective, responsive communication.
Following a referral, the CHW or Program Manager contacts the referring social worker to
confirm case context, goals, and any special considerations. Updates are shared via Apricot
or another County-approved system, including enrollment, attendance, and participation

5

notes. FYC is notified directly of urgent issues, and staff participate in CFTs, TDMs, and
other coordination meetings as needed.
The Program Manager serves as the primary liaison, facilitating clear internal
communication across CHWs, trainers, and clinicians to support continuity and
accountability.
c. Impact on Families
The Parent Ed program equips caregivers of adolescents and transitional-age youth,
including teen parents, with practical tools to create safe, stable, and developmentally
supportive home environments. By focusing on parenting teens, the program fills a gap in
traditional parent education models that primarily emphasize early childhood.
Caregivers strengthen skills in communication, boundary-setting, emotional regulation, and
supporting adolescent mental health. Trauma‑informed content helps caregivers
understand teen behavior in the context of brain development, risk-taking, and past
trauma—reducing conflict and strengthening parent‑child relationships.
For system‑involved families, the program intentionally reinforces Strengthening Families
Protective Factors, including parental resilience, parenting knowledge, social connections,
and healthy relationships. Progress toward these Protective Factors is reviewed throughout
the program and documented at completion, supporting case plan continuity, reunification
efforts, and next steps in collaboration with the referring FYC social worker.
d. Staff Competencies & Job Descriptions
Hanna Center’s parent education program will be delivered by a team of clinical and
training staff experienced in trauma-informed care, adolescent development, and family
engagement. CHWs and facilitators will receive structured onboarding and ongoing
professional development through both Hanna’s internal R&T department and external
EBP trainers.
Key competencies among staff include:
● Experience working with parents and caregivers of transitional-age youth (ages
12+)
● Comfort and skill in addressing topics related to teen mental health,
boundary-setting, and adolescent behavior
● Cultural and linguistic competence; at least one facilitator in each course cycle will
be bilingual in English and Spanish
● Familiarity with trauma-responsive facilitation strategies and adult learning theory
● Beginning in Spring 2026, staff from Hanna’s MHH will also be trained in
Motivational Interviewing (MI). While MI is not a required element for this service
area, its alignment with trauma-informed communication and goal-setting strategies
makes it a valuable addition to Hanna’s engagement toolkit.

E. Organizational Narrative
1. Organizational Structure
a. Service Availability and Physical Site
Hanna Center offers flexible scheduling to support working caregivers and those navigating
child welfare, probation, or school-related obligations. Parenting courses are primarily held
on weekday evenings (5:00–8:00 p.m.), with staff available during standard business hours
for referrals, intake, and coordination with County partners.
The program is based at Hanna’s 150-acre Sonoma Valley campus, which features
ADA-compliant, trauma-informed meeting rooms, bilingual reception, and secure spaces

7

suited for group facilitation and family engagement. The site exceeds County requirements
and is:
● Accessible by car and public transit
● Staffed by bilingual, trauma-informed personnel
● Equipped for hybrid delivery with Wi-Fi and tech support
To improve accessibility, Hanna also partners with local schools, hospitals, and health
centers for off-site delivery. Virtual participation is available for families who prefer or
require remote access.
b. Trauma-Informed Approach and Addressing Disproportionate Outcomes
Hanna Center’s parent education model recognizes that many caregivers in the child
welfare system have experienced trauma shaped by systemic inequities. The program
acknowledges the disproportionate impact on BIPOC, low-income, immigrant, and
system-involved families and is designed to reduce barriers and support healing.
Courses are delivered by bilingual, bicultural staff trained in trauma-informed engagement,
Motivational Interviewing, and culturally responsive facilitation. Rather than pathologizing
parenting challenges, Hanna meets caregivers with empathy, helping them build skills while
honoring their lived experience.
By combining evidence-based curricula like Triple P with individualized goals and
protective factor tracking, Hanna promotes practical, empowering change. This
trauma-informed, equity-centered approach strengthens caregiver confidence, family
relationships, and youth safety and stability.

d. Program Oversight and Staff Qualifications
Hanna’s Parent Ed program is overseen by a Program Manager with experience in contract
compliance, case coordination, and outcome reporting. Serving as the County liaison, the
Program Manager ensures fidelity to curriculum, timely documentation, and clear
communication.
The role is supported by Hanna’s MHH, led by a Clinical Director (LCSW) with 15+ years in
behavioral health and child welfare, who provides clinical supervision and ensures
alignment with trauma-informed care. The Program Manager also works closely with the
Vice President of Research & Training, who leads curriculum implementation, facilitator
training, and fidelity monitoring. The VP brings expertise in adult learning, restorative
practice, and systems transformation.
Together, this leadership team brings:
● Oversight of multi-year County contracts
● Proficiency with County systems (e.g., Apricot)
● Depth in trauma-informed and equity-based practice

● Cross-functional coordination across clinical, education, and CHW teams
The team meets regularly to monitor progress and maintain alignment with County
expectations.
e. Evidenced-Based Practice Implementation and Fidelity Monitoring
Beginning in 2026, Hanna Center will adopt Triple P – Positive Parenting Program as the
core curriculum for Parent Ed. In preparation, clinicians, CHWs, and educators are being
trained to ensure sufficient coverage and scheduling flexibility. Until fully implemented, the
R&T team will adapt a curriculum that blends Triple P principles with Hanna’s
trauma-informed practices.
Courses will be co-facilitated by trained clinicians and educators and delivered in person,
virtually, or at partner sites. Bilingual staff and materials will be available in English and
Spanish.
The Program Manager will oversee scheduling, registration, and coordination with FYC to
ensure curriculum alignment with case plans.
Fidelity will be maintained through session observations, facilitator self-assessments,
group supervision, and quarterly reviews led by R&T using Triple P standards. Evaluation
will be conducted by Hanna’s R&E team, tracking attendance, satisfaction, and pre/post
outcomes. Results will be shared with County partners to support quality improvement and
accountability.
2. Financial Position
a. Federal Funding Experience
Hanna Center has extensive experience managing federal and pass-through funds, including
contracts with the County of Sonoma supporting behavioral health, juvenile justice, and
family services. Oversight is shared among the Grants, Finance, and Program departments,
following internal protocols for cost allowability, procurement, and reporting as outlined in
Hanna’s Grant Management Policy.
Hanna complies with 2 CFR Part 200 (Uniform Guidance), including standards for cost
allocation, time/effort documentation, and subrecipient monitoring. Compliance is
supported by monthly reconciliations, quarterly grant reviews, and annual independent
audits. Hanna is fully prepared for a cost-reimbursement contract, with systems in place for
fiscal monitoring, documentation, and the 15% indirect cost cap. These systems align with
federal expectations under PSSF (ALN 93.556).

b. Internal Controls and Separation of Duties
Hanna maintains strong internal controls aligned with GAAP and Uniform Guidance.
