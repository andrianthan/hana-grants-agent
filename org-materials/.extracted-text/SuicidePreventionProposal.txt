reach across diverse audiences.

E. Quality Control
Hanna Center defines quality as consistent fidelity to evidence-based models, safe and culturally
responsive facilitation, and demonstrable learner outcomes supported by a continuous
improvement loop. Hanna’s quality control system includes:
1. Fidelity Procedures: Use of standardized instructor guides and session checklists aligned
to each model; pre-session facilitator alignment (objectives, audience needs, and safety
considerations); and post-session debriefs to capture implementation notes and
improvement opportunities. Periodic observation and review will be conducted to
ensure consistent delivery and adherence to model requirements.
2. Participant Competency and Application Checks: Where appropriate and consistent
with model guidelines, trainings will include brief pre/post measures of knowledge and
confidence as well as scenario-based application prompts (e.g., recognizing warning
signs, initiating a direct conversation, making a warm referral, and documenting next
steps).
3. Safe Messaging and Stigma Reduction: Hanna will ensure facilitation and materials use
best-practice suicide prevention language and avoid stigmatizing or unsafe terminology.
Trainers are prepared to manage sensitive discussions in a manner that supports hope,
help-seeking, and psychological safety for participants.
4. Corrective Action Loop: Participant feedback and outcome data are reviewed on a
rolling basis, with structured biannual reviews. When a pattern is identified (e.g., lower
confidence among a particular sector, geography, or priority population), Hanna
implements documented adjustments and reports changes and results back to the
County through quarterly dashboards.

in cultural humility, historical trauma awareness, and practices aligned with Sonoma County’s
Suicide Prevention Plan. Our trainers have extensive experience serving high-need populations,

Program Summary
Hanna Center Suicide Prevention Training Plan and Program
Hanna Center proposes to serve as Sonoma County’s neutral, countywide implementation
partner for the Suicide Prevention Training Plan and Program. The goal is to equip Sonoma
County residents, service providers, and behavioral health professionals with skills to identify
and respond to individuals at risk of suicide.
Over the ﬁve-year project period, Hanna will coordinate, deliver, and evaluate the Countydesignated evidence-based suicide prevention trainings—QPR and Mental Health First Aid
(Years 1–5), and AMSR and CALM (Years 2–5)—with ﬁdelity, cultural responsiveness, and
minimal administrative burden on County staﬀ. The plan and program will be aligned with the
Sonoma County Suicide Prevention Strategic Plan and Preliminary Training Outline.
Key strengths include: (1) deep Sonoma County presence and trust as a local entity; (2)
experienced certiﬁed instructors and licensed clinical oversight; (3) an internal Research &
Evaluation Department capable of producing dashboards and continuous improvement loops;
and (4) a collaborative approach aligned with the Life Worth Living Alliance and designed to
avoid duplication and strengthen the existing training ecosystem.
Hanna’s implementation model builds durable infrastructure beyond the contract term through
a coordinated instructor cohort, standardized training pathways by sector, and reusable
evaluation tools that strengthen countywide readiness and suicide-safer systems. The program
will prioritize outreach to populations disproportionately aﬀected by suicide, including BIPOC
communities, LGBTQ+ youth, veterans, older adults, and rural residents.
Hanna can initiate implementation within three months of contract execution using existing
training operations, instructor capacity, and evaluation infrastructure already in place.
Through strong infrastructure, meaningful community partnerships, and measurable outcomes,
Hanna Center is committed to building a sustainable framework for long-term suicide
prevention across Sonoma County.
