## Programs

### 1. Community Mental Health Hub (opened 2022)
- On-campus clinic open to all Sonoma County youth and families
- Bilingual (Spanish/English), culturally responsive
- 7 days/week
- **FY24-25:** 392 unique clients (57% increase YoY), 3,976 hours of bilingual therapy (90% increase)
- 48% youth ages 10-18, 61% Latine
- Services: individual therapy, family counseling, grief/bereavement, support groups, early psychosis intervention
- Modalities: TF-CBT, Motivational Interviewing, FFT, MST, Play/Art therapy, Solution-focused therapy, CBT, DBT
- 87% of CSC direct service staff are bilingual

### 2. Residential Program / Transitional Housing (launched April 2023)
- Transitional Housing Placement Program for foster youth ages 16-21
- Capacity: up to 36 youth (both young men and women)
- **FY24-25:** 53 young people housed
- Every minor aging out of foster care opted to remain in extended support
- Services: counseling, life-skills coaching (ILP: nutrition, cooking, laundry, bills), family/group therapy, vocational education
- 2024: expanded to include Hanna Academy students (IEP/behavioral challenges)

### 3. Hanna Academy (separate 501(c)(3))
- Non-public high school for students ages 14-18 with IEPs for emotional/behavioral challenges
- ~60 students/year
- Small class sizes, individualized curriculum
- CTE tracks: Construction Trades, Health, Technology for Industry, Culinary/Hospitality, Digital Photography/Design (new), 3D Modeling (new)
- WASC accredited, CA Dept of Education certified
- On-site Career Center for internships (opened 2023)
- 2024: opened residential program for up to 24 students

### 4. Hanna Institute (est. 2016)
- Training and research arm focused on trauma-informed care
- **FY24-25:** 2,419 professionals trained
- **Since June 2023:** ~6,801 participants across 339 trainings
- Coverage: Sonoma, Napa, Marin counties (and nationally)
- Evidence-based models: MHFA, Suicide Prevention Basics, Safety Planning, TIC, De-escalation, SEL
- Expanding to: QPR, AMSR, CALM certifications
- Annual Trauma-Informed Care Summit
- 30,000+ recipient mailing list
- Internal Research & Evaluation Department (uses Qualtrics)

### 5. Recreation & Enrichment
- **FY24-25:** 2,654 community members accessed recreation/support services
- Summer camps grew from 6 to 13 sessions (218 camp participants ages 6-13)
- ~1,700 participants utilize fields annually (soccer and football)
- Programs: sports, arts, gardening, robotics, culinary, life-skills workshops
- Financial assistance: 21% summer participants, 65% school-year participants

### 6. CSC Early Psychosis Program (launched Oct 20, 2025)
- Part of California's CYBHI / EPI-CAL initiative
- $2M state grant (Contract 1040.0701)
- 11 staff positions (10 of 11 filled as of Feb 2026)
- Fully staffed as of February 2026
- Serves children and adults with recent psychosis symptoms (ages 5+)
- Data collection via Beehive platform

### 7. HEAL Program / Camino Nuevo (formerly SV4HJ)
- Funded by Elevate Youth California ($550,000, Nov 2023 - Nov 2026)
- Also funded by S.H. Cowell Foundation ($40,000 for 2025-2026)
- Partner: On the Margins (curriculum co-developer)
- Target: 100-125 youth over 5 cohorts; 10 Spanish-speaking newcomer youth per Camino Nuevo cohort
- Healing Justice framework, delivered in Spanish
- Annual Healing Justice Resource Fair

### 8. School-Based Mental Health (ARPA-funded)
- Sonoma County ARPA grant: $613,690 (Feb 2024 - Mar 2026)
- Target: 250 students receiving mental health services, 1,000 receiving wellness support
- Suicide prevention awareness for 7th, 9th, 11th graders in SVUSD
- Substance use prevention groups, grief services, SEL curriculum
- Partners: SVUSD, Boys and Girls Club (2 locations)
