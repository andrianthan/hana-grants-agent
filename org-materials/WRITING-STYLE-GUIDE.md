## Tone & Voice

- **Professional, warm, mission-driven** — formal enough for funders but not cold
- Emphasizes **equity, cultural responsiveness, and relationship-based care**
- Frequently contextualizes need with data (MUP/HPSA designations, "1 in 5 teens" stat)
- Frames unrestricted/general operating support as powering "the full ecosystem"
- Heavy use of **specific data points** to demonstrate impact
- Budget narratives are brief and direct, leading with the largest line item

### Tone Calibration by Funder Type
- **Government RFPs** (FYC, Suicide Prevention, Probation): Most formal, compliance-heavy, acronym-dense, structured around RFP sections. Uses "fidelity," "continuous improvement loop," "durable infrastructure beyond contract term," "minimal administrative burden on County staff"
- **Foundation grants** (J.W. Couch, S.H. Cowell, Kimball): Warmer narrative, more aspirational, outcome-focused but less jargon. Uses "lifeline," "transform wellness from a concept into a lived practice"
- **Small community grants** (Jack London Yacht Club, Bank of Marin): Most accessible, community-oriented language, emphasizes local impact and partnerships. Uses "the effects ripple outward," "making all of us more effective, together"

## Structural Patterns

### Narrative Structure (Grant Applications)
1. **Open with founding story** — "Founded in 1945..." appears in nearly every application
2. **Establish need** with local data (MUP/HPSA designation, suicide stats, ACEs)
3. **Program description** with specific services and modalities listed
4. **Outcomes/metrics** — always include specific numbers, YoY comparisons when available
5. **Sustainability** — explain how program continues after grant period
6. **Equity statement** — highlight bilingual services, BIPOC staff, cultural responsiveness

### Common Data Points Cited
- Founded 1945, 75+ years of service
- 5,000+ people served annually
- 392 unique mental health clients (57% increase YoY)
- 3,976 hours of bilingual therapy (90% increase)
- 53 foster/TAY youth housed
- 2,654 community members in recreation
- 2,419 professionals trained in TIC
- 1 in 5 local teens has seriously considered suicide
- Sonoma Valley = MUP + HPSA
- 60% BIPOC, 70% low-income
- 50%+ residential youth have 4+ ACEs
- 87% of CSC staff are bilingual

### Budget Presentation
- Lead with total program cost
- Break into salary/benefits, direct program costs, indirect costs
- Indirect cost rate: typically **15%**
- Always note what the grant covers vs. what other funding covers
- For equipment: itemized tables with unit costs and quantities

## Formatting Conventions

- **Bullet-point lists** for metrics and outcomes (not paragraph form)
- **Tables** for budget breakdowns
- **Bold** key phrases and program names on first mention
- **Percentages** preferred over fractions for demographic data
- **Year-over-year comparisons** whenever possible (e.g., "57% increase YoY")
- Program names capitalized: Community Mental Health Hub, Career Center, Transitional Housing Program
- Contact info consistently formatted: Name, Title, email, phone
